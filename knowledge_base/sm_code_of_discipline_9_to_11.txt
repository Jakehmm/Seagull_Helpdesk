PART VI
Code of Discipline

Section 9: Minor Offense Notice

A pupil / student, who is found guilty of any of the following offenses, will be served a Minor Offense Notice:

    Using someone else's ID card and claiming it as his / her own;

    Attending classes late;

    Having perennial unexcused absences;

    Leaving the classroom without permission;

    Showing up late on the scheduled date and time of contest/activity/ seminar without valid reason;

    Littering;

    Displaying improper behavior such as sleeping, slouching, playing, talking, eating and drinking during school assemblies/functions;

    Submitting late or not submitting official documents on the dead line set by the school;

    Displaying rowdy behavior;

    Using foul / vulgar / obscene/ abusive or insulting language;

    Making unwanted noise, running, loitering in the corridor during class hours or displaying actions that disrupt / disturb classes or activities;

    Playing harmful jokes / dangerous games;

    Spreading rumors;

    Posting unofficial notices on campus walls and doors;

    Tampering / Defacing / Altering notices posted on bulletin boards;

    Entering the opposite sex's toilet;

    Tampering with other people's belongings;

    Disregarding proper grooming and uniform regulations, (including the wearing of make-up/colored powder and nail polish);

    Wearing inappropriate accessories;

    Chewing gum;

    Disregarding laboratory / gymnasium / classroom rules and regulations;

    Using school facilities such as tv-com, air conditioning unit, elevator, etc. without permission;

    Entering restricted areas in the campus;

    Entering the classroom / laboratory / gymnasium / auditorium / AVR during unauthorized periods;

    Other offenses analogous to any of the above.
    N.B.: Depending on the gravity of the offense, a minimum of one (1) point to a maximum of six (6) points will be deducted from the conduct grade.

Section 10: Major Offense Notice

A pupil / student, who is found guilty of any of the following, will be served a Major Offense Notice:

    Cutting classes;

    Failing to report to school / class / school activity without valid reason;

    Leaving the school during class hours without permission;

    Bullying / Intimidating / Annoying another student;

    Challenging / Provoking other student/s to a fight;

    Displaying inappropriate affection in public;

    Cyberbullying;

    Using information and communication technologies, such as email, cell phone, text messages, instant messages, defamatory personal websites, blog to support deliberate, repeated, and hostile behavior of an individual or group intending to harm others;

    Using scandalous words, expressions, statements, spreading malicious talk in any social media and other electronic media device, posting and reposting scandalous and gross materials / pictures;

    Posting unwarranted screenshot, which maligns the dignity and reputation of others;

    Showing defiance to any school personnel and / or person in authority;

    Instigating, leading or participating in concerted activities leading to the disruption of classes;

    Engaging in bribery;

    Committing perjury or showing non-cooperation in school investigation;

    Soliciting money or other instruments used as money;

    Taking part or directly involving in the malversation of class or school funds;

    Stealing or an attempt thereof;

    Using the school's name for any purpose without permission;

    Taking part in the destruction of school property (N.B.: Aside from an appropriate sanction, a pupil / student, who is found guilty of vandalism will be required to pay the damages.);

    Engaging in a brawl (whether inside or outside the school premises);

    Showing discourtesy or impoliteness to any person in authority;

    Inflicting physical injury upon another / other person/s (whether inside or outside the school premises);

    Attempting to or assaulting directly any member of the school community, visitors or guests;

    Gambling in any form inside or outside the school premises;

    Smoking and drinking alcoholic beverages inside or outside the school premises;

    Molesting, harassing or insulting any member of the school officials, faculty, staff or schoolmates;

    Coming to school under the influence of alcohol;

    Attempting to access or accessing adult-themed websites;

    Drawing any representations of obscene or pornographic acts;

    Carrying or possessing firecrackers inside and outside the school premises (N.B.: These items will be confiscated and will never be returned.);

    Forging parent's / guardian's signature; and

    Cheating or an attempt thereof.
    The following are considered as cheating:

    Possessing or using crib notes, which may be written on paper, on the desk or on any instruments, which have been banned from the examination;

    Copying or looking at another pupil's / student's paper;

    Holding up one's test paper or allowing another pupil / student to copy from his / her paper or writing on another pupil's / student's paper;

    Giving signals or any form of communication, which may be interpreted as giving or soliciting help during quizzes / examinations;

    Stealing examination paper;

    Disclosing examination items, including giving or receiving examination paper;

    Involving directly or indirectly in the leakage of examination paper;

    Plagiarizing, / claiming / passing off as one's own work the work of another;

    Altering test scores / answers after the return of test papers; and

    Other offenses analogous to any of the above.
    N.B.: For major offenses, a minimum of seven (7) points to a maximum of twelve (12) points may be deducted from the conduct grade. Suspension, exclusion or expulsion may also be imposed on the offending pupil / student.

Section 11: Offenses Warranting Immediate Exclusion/Expulsion
Automatic exclusion or expulsion will be imposed on a pupil/student found guilty of any of the following:

    Possessing, selling or using prohibited drugs on or off-campus;

    Organizing, recruiting, participating in any fraternity / sorority;

    Having committed or being involved in a crime (N.B.: A pupil / student who is under litigation for a crime will be excluded / dropped from the roll.);

    Forging or falsifying and / or tampering with academic or official records and documents;

    Making false statement of any material fact;

    Practicing or attempting to practice any deception or fraud;

    Showing gross acts of disrespect, in words or in deeds, which tend to put any school official or member of the faculty and staff in ridicule or contempt;

    Having shown any act that brings the name of the school into disrepute;

    Carrying or possessing explosives and dangerous weapons inside and outside the school premises;

    Engaging in illicit / scandalous behavior inside or outside the school premises;

    Involving in extortion;

    Engaging in arson;

    Having proven to be pregnant;

    Contracting marriage; and

    Other acts of commission / omission analogous to any of the above.

N.B.: (1) An offense may be given a higher sanction due to the following conditions:
a. Repeating the same offense anytime within the student's stay in the grade school / high school;
b. Committing offense aimed at any member of the school community;
c. Showing a pattern of behavior indicating a negative attitude toward school, school rules or school authorities; and
d. other situations as determined by the school authorities.

    School authorities may impose a lesser sanction or even suspend the sanction based on the following factors: Having proven
    a. good faith (an honest mistake);
    b. provocation from other parties;
    c. good disciplinary record;
    d. cooperation on the part of the student and his / her family;
    e. other situations determined by the school authorities.
