PART II
Academic Standards

Section 8: Promotion, Retention and Remedial Classes

    A pupil / student is promoted to the next grade level if he/she passes the desired requirements of all subject areas obtaining a grade of at least 75%.

For GS & JHS:

    Students who obtain 1 or 2 failing grades in any subject areas will be required to take remedial classes.

    Students who obtain 3 failing grades in any subject areas will be retained in the same grade level.

    Students who fail in any component subject, will be required to take remedial classes.

For SHS:

    Students who fail in any subject area must retake the subject failed in remedial class or have it as a back subject.

Section 9: Academic Probation

This is a restraining measure imposed by the school on pupils / students, who fail to meet the academic requirements of the previous school year. Its objective is to oblige the pupils / students to concentrate more on improving their academic performance.

    A new pupil / student shall be placed under academic probation if he / she fails in any subject at the end of his / her first year.

    A pupil / student shall be placed under academic probation if he / she is retained in the same grade level.

Section 10: Requirements for Graduation

    A pupil / student who has satisfactorily completed the requirements of the Grade School / Senior High School curriculum is eligible for graduation.

    A pupil / student who is required to take remedial classes may attend the moving-up/completion ceremony as part of the audience. Furthermore, he / she will not be issued his / her certificate and other credentials until all requirements of the course have been completed.

    A pupil / student is required to attend the graduation rehearsal. Otherwise, he/she will attend the actual ceremony only as part of the audience.

Section 11: Requirements for Moving-up/Completion

    A pupil / student who has satisfactorily completed the requirements for promotion of the Kinder curriculum and Junior High School curriculum is eligible for moving-up/completion.

    A pupil / student who is required to take remedial classes may attend the moving-up/completion ceremony as part of the audience. Furthermore, he / she will not be issued his / her certificate and other credentials until all requirements of the course have been completed.

    A pupil / student is required to attend the moving-up/completion rehearsal. Otherwise, he/she will attend the actual ceremony only as part of the audience.

Section 12: Physical Education

Each pupil / student is encouraged to increase his / her tendencies and abilities to play competitive recreational sports and games. Hence, all pupils / students are required to take Physical Education (P.E.) classes, which are held once a week.

    Pupils / Students are required to wear their P.E. uniforms during their scheduled P.E. days.

    Pupils / Students with health or physical disabilities may be exempted from joining strenuous physical activities. A request for exemption from the parents / guardians, supported by a certification from a physician, must be submitted to the Office of the Vice President before an exemption order will be issued. Pupils / Students falling under this category are still required to be physically present in his / her P.E. class. Tasks will be assigned by the teacher accordingly.
